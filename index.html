ABOUT US

CPCR'25

CPCR'25 brings together eminent scientists, academicians, and bright young minds to discuss the evolving landscape of chemical research in India and beyond. This two-day event will feature keynote lectures, oral presentations, poster sessions, and opportunities to network with leaders in the field.

Aims & Objectives

    The seminar is envisioned as a distinguished forum where leading scholars, researchers, and professionals from diverse fields of chemistry converge to present pioneering discoveries and highlight emerging frontiers; with keynote lectures, invited talks, scientific and student poster sessions, it offers a panoramic view of current research while encouraging cross-disciplinary dialogue, fostering intellectual exchange, mentorship, and critical reflection by uniting established experts with aspiring researchers, providing students with opportunities to showcase their work, sharpen communication skills, and receive constructive feedback, while inspiring collaboration, innovation, and research-driven solutions to address global challenges in sustainability, energy, healthcare, and materials, ultimately serving as both a platform for dialogue and a celebration of scholarship that reaffirms chemistry’s vital role in advancing knowledge and shaping a sustainable, equitable future.

College & Department

St. Xavier's College, established in 1860 by the Society of Jesus, a Catholic minority religious body, gained affiliation with the University of Calcutta in 1862. In 2006, it became West Bengal's first Autonomous College under Calcutta University. The college holds an exceptional A++ rating with a CGPA of 3.77 out of 4 from NAAC and is currently ranked 8th in the NIRF. It has also achieved a Grade A from DBT, with recommendations for the DBT Star College Scheme in Chemistry, Microbiology, and Physics. The Department of Chemistry is one of the oldest departments of the college and has benefitted from the grants of DST-FIST, UGC-CPE, DBT-STAR College Scheme as well as from the major research grants of the faculty members. Among the various academic activities conducted by this department, a yearly seminar/conference is always given a priority. Students are involved in different committees in such events and this is a training for them to move forward. Furthermore, it provides an exposure to the young talented minds to contemporary research and they will get an opportunity to interact with eminent scientists.
